Why Ask Take GO?

Plan Your Equipment Remarketing Investment Recovery

No planning.

Need for transparency of sale.

Remarketing and management of equipment is not the core business.

There isn’t a single place for all parties to create and execute their plan for equipment remarketing.

Logistical, communication, demand forecast challenges, equipment availability and the time it takes to dispose of the equipment have long plagued the investment recovery process.

Additionally, there is a need for transparency in the process of sale.

Remarketing equipment may not be the core business of the company.

The re-marketing efforts are focused on advertising, individual selling, or auctioning the equipment. The process has to be managed by each participant individually. Consequently, we see issues with lower recovery numbers, last-minute disposal through any means, and surplus inventory remaining at the end of the recovery period.

Sellers need to remarket equipment to recuperate capital which means needing to locate buyers.

Buyers need added services in order to be able to purchase the equipment.

Can this process be simplified and managed in one place in order to make the experience of selling and buying? There isn’t a single place for all parties to create and execute their plan to buy, sell, or service the equipment.

Our Background

We have extensive experience working with strategic planning, re-marketing, and trading (buy, sell) small to large fleets of surplus equipment. We understand the end-to-end process of successful remarketing deal management.

Product Value

Easy-to-use, planned, and transparent, we organize the equipment remarketing deal management process, improving efficiencies to predictably close deals so you can plan your equipment inventory lifecycle.
